HOW TO PLAY
The Goal

Move all 52 cards to the four foundation piles — one per suit — built from Ace up to King. Beat the time limit to earn ELO and climb the ranks.

The Tableau

The seven columns in the middle. Stack cards in alternating colours, descending order — red on black, black on red. A red 6 goes on a black 7. Only a King (or a stack starting with a King) can fill an empty column.

The Stock & Waste

Tap the stock pile (↺) to deal one card to the waste pile. Play the top waste card onto the tableau or foundation. When the stock runs out, tap again to recycle.

Moving Cards

Drag any face-up card or stack to a valid destination. Double-tap a card to auto-move it. The ✦ Finish button appears when the win is guaranteed — tap to complete instantly.

The Clock

Each game has a time limit based on your rank. Beat it and earn ELO — faster wins earn more. Run out of time and you lose ELO. The bar at the top of the game shows your remaining time.

Ranks

Everyone starts in Bronze I. Climb through the tiers by beating the clock. Each rank has tighter time limits and tougher seeds.

Promotion

Each rank has 3 sub-divisions (I, II, III) except Grand Master. Hit enough ELO and you're promoted automatically. Drop below the threshold and you're demoted. Climb high enough and you reach Grand Master — the hardest seeds, tightest time, no sub-divisions.

How does the rank system work?▾
Everyone starts at Bronze I with 0 ELO. Beat the clock to earn ELO. Hit the next threshold and you're promoted. The full ladder is Bronze → Silver → Gold → Diamond → Grand Master. Each rank below Grand Master has three sub-divisions (I, II, III).
How is ELO calculated?▾
Win and you earn between +20 and +60 ELO depending on how fast you finished — instant wins earn the most. Run out of time and you lose 15 ELO. Your ELO never goes below 0.
Do seeds get harder at higher ranks?▾
Yes. Bronze and Silver use straightforward layouts. Gold seeds require more sequence-building in the opening. Diamond and Grand Master use deals with fewer accessible cards and tougher tableau structures. Every seed is still guaranteed solvable.
Can I play unlimited games?▾
Yes. Play as many ranked games as you want. Every game is a fresh seed appropriate for your current rank.
What happens if I run out of time?▾
The game ends and you lose 15 ELO. You can immediately start a new game to try again. Time limits are strict — they're what makes the rank meaningful.
Can I get demoted?▾
Yes. If your ELO drops below the threshold for your current rank, you'll be demoted to the rank below. The system rewards consistent performance, not just lucky wins.
What is the Auto-Finish button?▾
When all remaining cards are face-up and the win is mathematically guaranteed, the ✦ Finish button appears. Tap to complete the game instantly. Your time stops the moment you tap it — useful for shaving seconds off your finish.
Are the deals always solvable?▾
Yes. Every seed is validated before use — checked for accessible Aces, colour balance, and existing tableau sequences. You will never receive an impossible deal.
Do I need an account?▾
You can play as a guest, but creating an account (just username + 4-digit PIN) saves your ELO and rank between sessions. Without an account your progress resets each time.

Daily Solitaire — Free Online Klondike Card Game

One new deal every day. Same cards for every player. See exactly where you rank. No ads. No downloads. No sign-up. Just solitaire — the way it should be.

What makes this different from every other online solitaire game

Most free solitaire games online are bloated with banner ads, cookie pop-ups, and auto-playing video ads that slow everything down. Daily Solitaire loads instantly, runs at 60fps on any device, and has zero advertising. The entire game runs in your browser — there is nothing to install and nothing to sign up for.

The competitive twist: every player on the same day receives an identical shuffled deck, generated from a deterministic daily seed. That means when you finish, your score is ranked against a real pool of other players who completed the same deal under the same conditions. Your percentile ranking is not a meaningless number — it reflects exactly how you performed versus everyone else who played that day.

How to play Klondike solitaire

Klondike is the classic solitaire variant — seven tableau columns, a stock pile, a waste pile, and four foundation piles. The goal is to move all 52 cards to the foundations, built by suit from Ace to King.

Cards in the tableau are stacked in alternating colours and descending rank order — a red 7 goes on a black 8, a black Queen goes on a red King. Only Kings can start a new empty column. When you run out of moves in the tableau, draw from the stock pile. Recycle the waste pile when the stock is empty, but doing so costs points.

How scoring works

Points are earned for every meaningful action:

  • +15 points — moving a card from the waste pile to a foundation
  • +10 points — moving a card from the tableau to a foundation
  • +5 points — moving a card within the tableau
  • +5 points — flipping a face-down card
  • −100 points — recycling the waste pile back through the stock
  • −15 points — using undo

When you complete the deal, two bonuses are added. A time bonus worth up to 700 points, which decreases by 2 for every second over 60 seconds. And a move efficiency bonus worth up to 300 points, which decreases by 3 for every move over 80. Complete the game quickly and cleanly to maximise your score.

Is the daily deal always solvable?

Yes. Every daily deal is validated by a solvability algorithm before it is used. The algorithm checks that the deck has accessible Aces, a balanced colour distribution in the stock, and existing alternating sequences in the initial tableau layout. You will never be given an impossible deal.

Can I play solitaire on my phone?

Yes. The game is built mobile-first with touch drag-and-drop using native pointer events. Cards are dynamically sized to fit any screen width — from small phones to large desktop monitors. On iOS you can add it to your home screen from Safari (Share → Add to Home Screen) and it runs like a native app.

Tips to rank higher

The players who crack the top 1% are efficient, not just fast. Speed matters but uncontrolled speed leads to mistakes and wastes undo credits. Some principles that help:

  • Uncover face-down cards as early as possible — each flip earns points and opens new moves
  • Avoid recycling the stock pile. Each recycle costs 100 points — often more than the card you were looking for is worth
  • Don't move cards to foundations too early if doing so blocks tableau sequences you need
  • Kings on empty columns should be Kings that have useful sequences — don't fill a column just because you can
  • Use undo sparingly — each one costs 15 points and the best players rarely need it

Frequently asked questions

Does everyone really get the same deal?

Yes. The shuffle uses a seeded random number generator where the seed is derived from the current date. Every player on the same calendar day gets an identical 52-card order. The seed changes at midnight.

Can I play yesterday's deal?

No. Each daily deal is only active for 24 hours and the ranking is calculated only against players who finish on the same day. This keeps the competition fair and time-limited.

What does "Top 1%" actually mean?

It means your final score was higher than 99% of all players who completed that day's deal. The ranking updates in real time as more people finish throughout the day — so your rank might improve even after you've played.

Is there a limit on how many times I can play?

One attempt per day, per device. This is what makes the daily ranking meaningful — everyone gets one shot.

What is the Auto-Finish button?

When all remaining cards are face-up and the game can be completed automatically, an Auto-Finish button appears. Tapping it plays out the remaining moves instantly. Your score and ranking are calculated the same way regardless of whether you finish manually or use Auto-Finish.
